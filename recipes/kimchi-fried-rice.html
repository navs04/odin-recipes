Kimchi Fried Rice

Description

Kimchi fried rice (Kimchi Bokkeumbap) is a bold, flavorful Korean dish that turns simple ingredients into a delicious, spicy, and umami-packed meal. Made with fermented kimchi, rice, and a few pantry staples, this dish is a go-to for quick, satisfying comfort food. The magic lies in the tangy, slightly sour depth of kimchi, which caramelizes beautifully when stir-fried, blending perfectly with the rice. A drizzle of sesame oil adds richness, while green onions and sesame seeds enhance the aroma. Topped with a fried egg, the creamy yolk balances the spiciness, creating an irresistible harmony of textures and flavors. Whether you're using day-old rice or fresh-cooked grains, this one-pan meal is easy, quick, and perfect for a busy day!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • ½ cup kimchi, chopped + 2 tbsp kimchi juice
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) (optional)
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1 egg (for topping)
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Sauté the Kimchi - Heat sesame oil in a pan, add chopped kimchi and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly caramelized.
  2. Add Rice & Seasonings - Stir in rice, kimchi juice, soy sauce, and gochugaru. Mix well and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  3. Top with Egg - Fry an egg separately and place it on top of the fried rice.
  4. Garnish & Serve - Sprinkle with green onions and sesame seeds. Serve hot and enjoy!
